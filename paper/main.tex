\begin{abstract}
Sparse-attention methods for autoregressive decode establish quality at moderate
context lengths but are rarely characterised at the production scales
($N\ge 10\text{K}$ tokens) where they would be deployed, and almost never validated
under a control for the magnitude of the claim being made. We assemble four
mutually reinforcing results around a single object --- the concentration of the
softmax attention distribution --- and validate every quantitative claim under a
matched-control discipline that we make explicit. First, the Q-axis ranking of
DCR-Attention is an \emph{exact} algebraic identity (Theorem~1), confirmed against
brute-force $QK^\top$ top-$k$ on $45/45$ configurations with mean Jaccard overlap
$\ge 0.998$. Second, Q-axis quality \emph{strengthens} with scale: relative
perplexity degradation drops to $\dPPL=+0.118\%$ at $N=8\text{K}$ with $50\%$
compute saving, which we ground in the sub-logarithmic growth of decode-attention
entropy, $H_N=\alpha\log N+\beta$ with measured $\alpha\approx 0.31$, formalised as
$\dPPL(N)=O\!\big(N^{-(1-\alpha)}\big)$ (Theorem~3, descriptive). Third, sparse
decode retains dependence on a distant required key with no measurable selectivity
loss versus full attention (paired-CI, wrong-needle differential
$\text{CI}_{\text{upper}}\le 0.05$) up to $N=128\text{K}$, and does so by
\emph{genuinely selecting} the needle; at extreme coverage the fact delocalises and
accuracy ceases to indicate the memory mechanism. Fourth, a per-query certificate
$\varepsilon\le 2W e^{H(\alpha(q))}/k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ supplies a checkable accuracy
guarantee with zero observed false negatives over $45$ calibration cells. A
multi-batch profiling study shows the Python reference path collapses between
batch~4 and~8 via an allocator-induced memory wall, establishing that a fused
Triton kernel (measured $1.21\times$ over SDPA for the ABKV variant) is a structural
prerequisite, not an optimisation. The methodology section reports a worked
matched-control \emph{null} that closed an earlier spectral-mechanism hypothesis,
and we carry the same discipline --- pre-registration, magnitude-matched
intervention, scope honesty, and an explicit retraction ledger --- throughout. All
claims are scoped to Llama-3.2-1B, WikiText-2, recency window $64$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Modern autoregressive language models operate over context windows of tens of
thousands of tokens, yet quality validation of sparse-attention methods has lagged:
published decode-quality results typically cap at $N\le 1024$ tokens. A method that
works at $N=500$ can silently fail at $N=8\text{K}$, and the ``obvious''
extrapolation can be quantitatively wrong in either direction. A second, subtler gap
is methodological: mechanistic and quantitative claims about attention are rarely
subjected to a control for the \emph{magnitude} of the intervention or the strength
of the assertion, and uncontrolled claims tend to confirm whatever hypothesis
motivated them.

\paragraph{Prior result we extend.} In earlier work we established Q-axis
rank-local sparse attention as the mathematically optimal axis choice for
autoregressive decode: for a decode query $Q$ and cached keys $\{K_j\}$, ranking by
the projection onto $\uQ=Q/\|Q\|_2$ is identical to ranking by attention score
(Theorem~1, restated in \S\ref{sec:theory}). That work validated the identity at
$N\le 500$ and reported a large quality advantage over a PCA-axis baseline, but
explicitly limited validation due to diagnostic memory constraints. Three
production-relevant questions remained open: (Q1) does the quality advantage scale
to long context? (Q2) does the baseline failure scale comparably or relax? (Q3) is
there a deployable latency operating point?

\paragraph{Contributions.} This paper answers all three and adds two results that
were not in scope before --- a long-range-retention characterisation and a per-query
correctness certificate --- assembled under a single explicit discipline. In order
of novelty:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{Exact ranking (Theorem~1).} A training-free identity, non-falsifiable
by ablation because it is algebra; empirically confirmed on $45/45$ brute-force
configurations (\S\ref{sec:scaling}).
\item \textbf{A measured scaling law (Theorem~3).} $\dPPL(N)=O(N^{-(1-\alpha)})$ for
measured $\alpha\approx0.31$, with two independent empirical confirmations; stated
as a \emph{description}, not a mechanism (\S\ref{sec:theory},
\S\ref{sec:scaling}).
\item \textbf{Retention of long-range dependence (\S\ref{sec:retention}).} A
pre-registered staircase of needle tasks shows sparse decode retains a distant
required key with no measurable selectivity loss up to $N=128\text{K}$ for
coverage $c\gtrsim0.10$, via true selection; and a leakage dissociation at extreme
coverage.
\item \textbf{Per-query certificate (\S\ref{sec:theory}).} A runtime-checkable bound
$\varepsilon\le 2W e^{H(\alpha(q))}/k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ with an exponential-decay
false-negative guarantee, plus an exact non-asymptotic error identity.
\item \textbf{A memory-wall systems result (\S\ref{sec:systems}).} The Python
masked-dense path fails between batch~4 and~8 through allocator fragmentation; the
ABKV fused kernel recovers a measured $1.21\times$ over SDPA.
\item \textbf{A matched-control methodology (\S\ref{sec:method}).} The discipline
that validates the above, with a worked null from a closed spectral programme and an
explicit retraction ledger (\S\ref{sec:limitations}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background and related work}
\label{sec:background}

\paragraph{Q-axis recap.} For a single decode step with query $Q\in\mathbb{R}^d$ and
cached keys $K\in\mathbb{R}^{N\times d}$, the attention output is
$\Attn(Q,K,V)=\softmax(QK^\top/\sqrt{d})\,V$. Q-axis rank-local attention sets the
ordering axis $\uQ=Q/\|Q\|_2$, sorts keys by the projection $K_j\!\cdot\!\uQ$, and
admits the top-$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ keys to the softmax. The DCR (Detect--Choose--Route)
dispatcher applies this rank-local primitive only when a coherence test on $Q$ passes
and otherwise falls back to dense attention.

\paragraph{Selection-based sparse attention.} The dominant decode-time family selects
a subset of keys per query: StreamingLLM (sink + recency), H2O (accumulated
attention), SnapKV (last-window scores), Quest (query-aware page min/max proxies),
InfLLM (block representative vectors), RetrievalAttention (CPU-resident ANN with an
OOD-aware bridge), and ParisKV (drift-robust, million-token). All are training-free;
DCR-Attention sits in this family but is the only member whose ranking criterion is
\emph{exact} (Theorem~1).

\paragraph{Trained sparse attention.} NSA learns the sparsity pattern jointly with
the model; DSA (DeepSeek-V3.2) trains a lightning indexer
$I_{t,s}=\sum_j w_{j}\,\mathrm{ReLU}(q_{j}\!\cdot\!k_{s})$ in a two-stage warm-up and
ships in production with kernel support; GLM-5 adopts the same paradigm; HISA
accelerates DSA with a hierarchical indexer. These reach better quality on their own
training distribution but require continued pre-training and extra parameters. Q-axis
is training-free and applies to any pre-trained checkpoint.

\paragraph{Low-rank and dimensional methods.} Loki exploits the empirically low
effective rank of key tensors ($\sim80$ of $128$) to cut data movement;
EigenAttention and EliteKV compress $K/V$ via SVD or RoPE-frequency selection; MLA
caches a single low-dimensional latent per token. A natural extension of Q-axis is to
operate in the top-$r$ PCA subspace of $K$, preserving exactly the inner product
ranked by Theorem~1 (\S\ref{sec:future}).

\paragraph{Theory of top-$k$ truncation.} Recent work derives the exact identity
$\TV(P,\hat P)=1-e^{-\mathrm{KL}(\hat P\|P)}$ and a head--tail decomposition that we
use in the proof of Theorem~3, and separately observes that low-entropy states are
better adapted to top-$k$ decoding --- qualitatively consistent with our
Assumption~2, but without a scaling law in $N$. We discuss precedence in
\S\ref{sec:distinction}.

\section{Theory: entropy concentration}
\label{sec:theory}

\subsection{Exact Q-axis ranking}

\begin{theorem}[Q-axis equivalence]
\label{thm:qaxis}
For any decode query $Q\in\mathbb{R}^d$ with cached keys
$\{K_j\}_{j=1}^N\subset\mathbb{R}^d$, the ordering induced by the projection axis
$\uQ:=Q/\|Q\|_2$ is identical to the ordering by attention score:
\[
\langle \uQ,K_i\rangle > \langle \uQ,K_j\rangle
\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
\langle Q,K_i\rangle > \langle Q,K_j\rangle .
\]
\end{theorem}

The statement is an algebraic identity: $\langle Q,\cdot\rangle=\|Q\|_2\,\langle
\uQ,\cdot\rangle$ and $\|Q\|_2>0$, so the two orderings coincide exactly (modulo ties
at \texttt{bf16} precision). It is therefore \emph{not} falsifiable by ablation; the
proof is recorded in Appendix~\ref{app:proof-thm1}. The practical consequence is that
the top-$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ keys selected by the $O(d)$ projection are exactly the
top-$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ keys by attention score, with no learning and no approximation.

\subsection{Entropy concentration and asymptotic strengthening}

Let $H_N=-\sum_i p_{N,i}\log p_{N,i}$ denote the attention entropy at a decode step
with cache size $N$, where $p_{N,i}=\softmax(Q\!\cdot\!K_i/\sqrt{d})_i$. Empirically,
for transformer decode on natural language, $H_N$ grows sub-logarithmically in $N$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:entropy}
H_N=\alpha\log N+\beta+o(1),\qquad \alpha<1.
\end{equation}
For Llama-3.2-1B on WikiText-2 we measure $\alpha\approx0.31$ averaged over heads and
decode positions $t\in[64,N]$, against the uniform-attention reference $\alpha=1$. The
effective number of relevant keys $\Keff(N):=e^{H_N}$ therefore grows much slower than
$N$: at $N=20\text{K}$ we measure $\Keff\approx87$, a sparsity ratio
$\Keff/N\approx0.4\%$.

\begin{assumption}[Entropy concentration]
\label{ass:entropy}
There exist $\alpha<1$ and $\beta\in\mathbb{R}$ such that the per-step attention
entropy satisfies $H_N=\alpha\log N+\beta+o(1)$ as $N\to\infty$.
\end{assumption}

\begin{theorem}[Asymptotic strengthening of Q-axis with adaptive coverage]
\label{thm:scaling}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:entropy} with $\alpha<1$, Q-axis rank-local attention with
adaptive coverage floor $c_{\mathrm{floor}}>0$ achieves perplexity degradation
$\dPPL(N)=O\!\big(N^{-(1-\alpha)}\big)$; the relative degradation strictly decreases
with $N$.
\end{theorem}

The proof (Appendix~\ref{app:proof-thm3}) admits $k_{\mathrm{eff}}=\lfloor
c_{\mathrm{floor}}N\rfloor$ keys; under Assumption~\ref{ass:entropy} the ratio
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}/\Keff\sim N^{1-\alpha}$ grows unboundedly, the captured probability
mass approaches $1$, and the truncated tail mass $\tau_N=\TV(P_N,\hat P_N)$ decays as
$O(N^{-(1-\alpha)})$ via the head--tail decomposition. For $\alpha=0.31$, Theorem~3
predicts $\dPPL(N)\propto N^{-0.69}$.

\begin{remark}[Head-wise non-vacuity]
The worst-head exponent $\alpha^*=\max_h\alpha_h$ controls the rate; for all twelve
heads of Llama-3.2-1B we measure $\alpha_h\in[0.22,0.39]$, so the guarantee is
non-vacuous head-wise.
\end{remark}

We emphasise that Theorem~\ref{thm:scaling} is stated as a \emph{description}: the
exponent $\alpha$ is measured per model, not derived from first principles, and the
result is not claimed as a mechanism. \S\ref{sec:method} explains why this scoping is
load-bearing, and \S\ref{sec:future} registers the matched-control causal test (Gap-1)
that will upgrade or honestly close it.

\paragraph{Causal status (pre-registered).} As of this release the premise of
Theorem~\ref{thm:scaling} --- that entropy concentration is causally load-bearing for
prediction --- has \emph{not} been subjected to a causal test; the claim is therefore
descriptive. The matched-control protocol that will settle it is pre-registered in
\S\ref{sec:future}, with both outcomes fixed in advance: a matched-magnitude null
finalises Theorem~\ref{thm:scaling} as descriptive, while a significant matched
differential upgrades it to causally grounded. We report the status this way so that
the released record stakes the causal-test \emph{design}, not its result.

\subsection{A per-query certificate and the exact error geometry}
\label{sec:certificate}

Beyond the asymptotic rate, a per-query bound gives a runtime-checkable guarantee. For
a query $q$ with rank-local entropy $H(\alpha(q))$ and top-$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ selection,
the rank-local attention error is bounded by
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:certificate}
\varepsilon \;\le\; 2W\,\frac{e^{H(\alpha(q))}}{k_{\mathrm{eff}}}
\;=\; 2W\exp\!\big(H(\alpha(q))-\log k_{\mathrm{eff}}\big),
\end{equation}
with $W$ a value-range constant. A dispatcher (Detector~v2,
Appendix~\ref{app:detector}) converts \eqref{eq:certificate} into a routing decision in
$O(Nd)$ overhead with an exponential-decay bound on the false-negative rate, validated
on $45$ calibration cells with \emph{zero} observed false negatives. Unlike fixed
sparse or low-rank schemes, the approximation carries a checkable per-input guarantee.

The bound is matched by an exact non-asymptotic identity. Writing $\gamma$ for the
renormalisation residual with $\sum_i\gamma_i=0$ and $K$ for the value-vector Gram
matrix, the rank-local output error satisfies, at every finite $k$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:vgram}
\varepsilon^2=\sum_{i,j}\gamma_i\gamma_j K_{ij}.
\end{equation}
A common-mode (rank-1) cancellation theorem then shows the rank-1 component of $K$
vanishes identically, yielding $\varepsilon^2=\sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}^2\,\psi(\alpha,k)+R$
with effective trace $\sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}^2=K_{ii}-\bar c$ rather than the naive
$\mathrm{tr}\,\Sigma$. We validate this refined formula on Llama-3.2-1B across $16$
layers and $4$ heads to within $0.5\%$ aggregate accuracy. A controlled negative result
(\S\ref{sec:limitations}, R4) establishes that the asymptotic tail-equivalence
$\psi(\alpha,k)\to\tau_k^2$ \emph{fails} at operational truncation budgets, so the
often-cited closed form $\alpha^*=r-\tfrac12$ is not predictive at any attainable $k$;
\eqref{eq:vgram} is used directly instead.

\section{Retention of long-range dependence}
\label{sec:retention}

Iso-perplexity does not imply iso-retrieval. We ask a distinct, load-bearing question:
does top-$k$ sparse decode preserve dependence on a distant key that the answer
actually requires? The study is a pre-registered staircase on a bit-for-bit restored
system (live correctness re-verified: logit $\Delta=0$, cache ratio $0.50783$,
$\dPPL=+0.714\%$). Two matched arms throughout: \textbf{DENSE} (all-SDPA, the
$c$-independent control) and \textbf{SPARSE} (Q-axis top-$k$, all-DCR). Route counters
are verified per cell so a silent SDPA fallback invalidates a cell; the prefill
confound is re-confirmed each run so that hits are decode-attributable. Fixed config:
$B=1$, greedy decode, \texttt{fp32} softmax, $k_{\text{window}}=64$, hero coverage
$c=0.15$.

\paragraph{Gate v1 --- single-needle (sanity).} One unique 7-digit code in homogeneous
filler; $N\in\{8\text{K},32\text{K},128\text{K}\}$, needle depth outside the recency
window. All twelve cells: $\text{dense}=\text{sparse}=1.000$. Top-$k$ does not drop a
sole salient needle. Necessary, not sufficient (no headroom).

\paragraph{Gate v2 --- exact-id multi-needle (uninformative by design).} Querying a
needle by a unique 4-digit id is a lexical escape hatch: distractors with different ids
do not compete, so selection under competition is never tested. Sparse was
byte-identical to dense. The design lesson dictated v3. (A methodological note: a pilot
cell read $0.80$ at $n=5$ and regressed to $0.99$ at $n=100$ --- a phantom boundary that
point-thresholding would have reported and that paired-CI plus a monotone-neighbour rule
correctly rejected.)

\paragraph{Gate v3 --- soft disambiguation (the gate that bit).} Three harder modes
force genuine selection: \emph{neardup} (near-duplicate ids, Hamming-1),
\emph{semantic} (no id in query; needle keyed by a unique attribute), and
\emph{multihop} (answer requires chaining). With paired bootstrap CI and a grid-aware
monotone verdict, the failure criterion is
$\text{CI}_{\text{upper}}(\text{dense}-\text{sparse correct})>0.05$ or the analogous
wrong-needle differential $>0.05$. Result: multihop \emph{floors} in all nine cells
(the 1B base cannot do implicit 2-hop in a forward pass --- a property of the base,
\emph{not} a sparse failure, excluded per pre-registration); neardup/semantic enter the
informative band at higher $K/N$ (dense $0.40$--$0.93$). On all ten informative cells:
\emph{zero} FAILs. In the hard cells dense itself has a high wrong-needle error rate,
and sparse \emph{reproduces dense's error rate and pattern}: the wrong-needle
differential, the core selectivity signal, has $\text{CI}_{\text{upper}}\le0.05$ in
every cell. \textbf{Verdict: ROBUST at $c=0.15$.}

\paragraph{Coverage sweep --- the boundary is in $N$, not $c$.} Holding the v3
informative cells and sweeping $c\in\{0.15,0.10,0.05,0.02\}$ on paired prompts, every
$N\le32\text{K}$ cell holds down to $c=0.02$, while the $N=131072$ cells fail. Two
findings, by confidence:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{(F1, confirmed)} Retention holds to $c\approx0.10$ on all $N$ up to
$128\text{K}$ via genuine selection: at $c=0.15$ correct answers occur \emph{because}
top-$k$ selects the needle.
\item \textbf{(F2, confirmed) Leakage dissociation.} At $c=0.02$ on $128\text{K}$, a
large fraction of \emph{correct} answers occur with the needle \emph{not} in top-$k$
(out-corr/correct up to $0.70$ on semantic/4). Accuracy ceases to be a valid indicator
of the memory mechanism: the fact has delocalised into other tokens during prefill.
This is a genuine mechanistic result with a direct compression corollary.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{What is deferred (in progress, not claimed).} The failure mode at the
$128\text{K}$ boundary is, on current data, \emph{starvation}-leaning (the starvation
differential CI excludes zero) while \emph{mis-selection} is not statistically
established --- the inverse of an earlier ``critical semantic capacity'' framing, which
does not survive (a cell with $k_{\mathrm{eff}}=19{,}666$ fails while one with
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}=1{,}602$ holds, so there is no absolute-$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ threshold).
We do \emph{not} report the boundary mechanism as decided: a causal oracle-inject probe
and an anti-inject validation of the needle-in-top-$k$ metric are required, together
with a multi-seed replication of the boundary and a TTFT/decode latency decomposition,
before the boundary is a publishable claim (\S\ref{sec:future}).

\paragraph{Honest one-paragraph claim.} On Llama-3.2-1B, Q-axis top-$k$ sparse decode
retains dependence on a distant required key with no measurable selectivity loss versus
full attention across single-needle, near-duplicate, and semantic multi-needle
competition, at context lengths to $128\text{K}$, for coverage $c\gtrsim0.10$, and does
so by genuinely selecting the needle. The retention boundary appears only at the
extreme of $c\approx0.02$ and only at $N=131072$; at that extreme accuracy dissociates
from the memory mechanism. All claims are at fixed $k_{\text{window}}=64$ on a single
base model; the synthesis (multi-hop) regime is untestable on this base.

\section{Systems: kernel design and the multi-batch wall}
\label{sec:systems}

\paragraph{Profiling the Python path.} At $N=20\text{K}$, $c_{\mathrm{floor}}=0.5$ the
Python masked-dense path totals $58$\,ms/step, dominated by the full $QK^\top$ plus mask
application ($\sim30$\,ms) --- wasteful, since $k_{\mathrm{eff}}=0.5N$ keys are discarded
post-softmax. SDPA measures $33$\,ms; a fused top-$k$ kernel targets $14$\,ms
($2.4\times$ over SDPA, $4.1\times$ over Python).

\paragraph{Per-pass profiling and a corrected bottleneck claim.} A per-pass profile on
the hero configuration L4 ($N=32\text{K}$, $B=4$, $c=0.15$) shows Pass-2
(\texttt{torch.argsort} stable, \texttt{fp32}) dominates at $74.4\%$ of end-to-end M4
(Table~\ref{tab:perpass}); Pass-3 is only $10.9\%$. This \emph{corrects} an earlier
hypothesis that flagged Pass-3 as the bottleneck (\S\ref{sec:limitations}, R8) and
reframes the kernel value proposition: eliminate the dominant Pass-2 stable-sort cost.

\paragraph{ABKV: a prefix kernel that eliminates decode-time selection.} The ABKV

bounds the worst case if a quality gate triggers.

\paragraph{Triple-tier validation.} We do not claim bitwise equivalence (\texttt{bf16}
reductions are non-associative). Acceptance requires all three tiers: (T1, numerical)
$|\Delta\text{NLL}/\text{tok}|\le5\times10^{-4}$ for $\ge99\%$ of steps over an
$N=5\text{K}$ trace, max $\le5\times10^{-3}$; (T2, statistical)
$|\mathrm{PPL}_{\text{kernel}}-\mathrm{PPL}_{\text{ref}}|/\mathrm{PPL}_{\text{ref}}\le0.05\%$
over $3$ runs; (T3, algorithmic) top-$k$ overlap $\ge0.999$, $\cos(u^{\text{kernel}}_Q,
u^{\text{ref}}_Q)\ge0.9999$, exact mask sparsity.

\paragraph{The multi-batch memory wall.} Profiling the Python DCR path against SDPA at

non-fused intermediate tensors (top-$k$ dispatch buffers, scatter/gather state,
masked-softmax intermediates) whose sizes vary with adaptive coverage, so PyTorch's
caching allocator cannot reclaim them across steps. SDPA, a single fused kernel, shows
no such pathology. The fused kernel of \S\ref{sec:systems} eliminates the entire
allocation chain. \textbf{The kernel rewrite is therefore a structural prerequisite for
production deployment, not an optimisation.}

\section{Methodology: matched-control discipline}
\label{sec:method}

The quantitative claims above are validated under a discipline we make explicit,
because it is the discipline --- not any single number --- that lets the claims survive.
It originates in a worked \emph{null} from a closed companion programme.

\paragraph{A worked example (the closed spectral programme).} The free-energy Hessian
of an attention head can be written $H=-\beta\,\Cov_p(k)$, and on trained models it is
typically spike-dominated. The hypothesis under test was that this dominant mode is a
privileged computational object whose removal should selectively damage next-token
prediction. The natural intervention --- project out the dominant direction, then
rescale to preserve $\|K\|_F$ --- preserves the \emph{wrong} invariant. Removing the
dominant direction deletes a large key component; removing a bulk direction deletes a
tiny one; rescaling to a common $\|K\|_F$ leaves a perturbation-norm asymmetry
$\|\delta K\|_F$ of $10$--$30\times$ (for one early-layer head, $M_T\approx84.9$ vs.\
$M_C\approx15.8$). An uncontrolled smoke run accordingly showed the treatment producing
$\sim18\times$ the control's KL --- ``a striking positive,'' and entirely consistent
with the dominant mode being computationally irrelevant. The asymmetry was caught not by
inspecting the result but by directly measuring $\|\delta K\|_F$ before the full run.

\paragraph{The three-component checklist.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{Matched-magnitude intervention.} Match the \emph{perturbation norm}
$\|\delta\|$ applied to the model --- not an indirect invariant such as $\|K\|_F$. We
scaled both treatment and control to $T=\min(M_T,M_C)$ per layer--head pair; the verified
treatment-to-control ratio was $1.000$ on every pair.
\item \textbf{A random-spectrum baseline.} Before attributing significance to a spectral
object, check whether a random spectrum exhibits the same property. A three-statistic
coupling that looked like trained structure was reproduced exactly by random spectra ---
an algebraic identity among monotone functions of spectral peakedness, not a property of
the network.
\item \textbf{Pre-registered outcome criteria.} Fix, before running, what each outcome
means, so a null is as reportable as a positive and there is no post-hoc freedom to
reinterpret.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Result of applying the framework.} On Llama-3.2-1B over WikiText-2 (twelve
layer--head pairs, $25$ prompts each, $300$ measurements, context $256$), the
matched-control ablation returned a pre-registered \emph{null}
(Table~\ref{tab:matched}): the treatment-minus-control next-token NLL differential is
$+2.72\times10^{-4}$, $95\%$ CI $[-1.7,7.0]\times10^{-4}$ bracketing zero, Wilcoxon
$p=0.26$. Suppressing the dominant spectral mode harms prediction no more than
suppressing a random mode of equal perturbation magnitude. Note that the \emph{KL}
differential \emph{is} significant ($p<10^{-38}$) --- a dissociation that itself argues
for choosing the operationally meaningful metric (NLL) before running.

\paragraph{Why this is the spine.} The lesson is reproducible, not philosophical: the
same attention geometry, on the same model and dataset, was \emph{falsified} as a
mechanism and \emph{survived} as a description and as algebra. We therefore apply the
checklist as the validation standard for every quantitative claim in this paper, and we
state in \S\ref{sec:future} the pre-registered matched-control test that would apply it
to Theorem~\ref{thm:scaling} itself.

\section{Distinction from concurrent work}
\label{sec:distinction}

The mainstream sparse-attention literature converges on ``token-relevance indexer +
top-$k$ selection + sparse downstream attention,'' differing in (a) whether the indexer
is trained or training-free and (b) whether the criterion has formal guarantees. Ours is
the only training-free path with an \emph{exact} ranking guarantee (Theorem~1) plus a
\emph{closed-form scaling law} (Theorem~3) and a \emph{per-query certificate}
(\S\ref{sec:certificate}). Theorem~1 is a special case of the MIPS-as-query-scaled-NNS
equivalence with $\uQ=Q/\|Q\|_2$ specific to attention. Concurrent theory derives a
TV-distance bound for top-$k$ truncation (which we use) and observes qualitatively that
low-entropy states suit top-$k$ decoding, but does not derive a scaling law in $N$; our
measured exponent $\alpha$ and the rate $O(N^{-(1-\alpha)})$ are the new content. We
note the precedence pressure honestly: the qualitative entropy$\leftrightarrow$top-$k$
observation is already public, which is part of why we release for priority now and scope
the scaling claim as descriptive pending the causal test.

\section{Limitations and retraction ledger}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Limitations.} Single model family (Llama-3.2-1B) and single dataset
(WikiText-2); $\alpha$ may differ across architectures and domains. Triton/ABKV figures
are partly projected from measured anchors; the memory-wall finding is preliminary and
requires the kernel rewrite to enable batched validation. Retention claims are at fixed
$k_{\text{window}}=64$ on one base; multihop floors on the 1B base. KV-cache memory is
not addressed (orthogonal and complementary to Q-axis).

\paragraph{Retraction ledger.} In the interest of an honest permanent record, we list
hypotheses entertained in the programme and \emph{retracted}, with how each fell:
\begin{description}[leftmargin=2.2em,itemsep=1pt]
\item[R1] Spectral geometry as a computational \emph{mechanism} (dominant mode
load-bearing) --- matched-magnitude null (\S\ref{sec:method}).
\item[R2] A universal U-shaped depth profile of spectral concentration --- did not
reproduce across five architectures; model-specific.
\item[R3] A non-trivial three-statistic spectral coupling --- reproduced by random
spectra; algebraic identity.
\item[R4] Tail-equivalence $\psi(\alpha,k)\to\tau_k^2$ and the closed form
$\alpha^*=r-\tfrac12$ --- not predictive at operational $k$ (\S\ref{sec:certificate}).
\item[R5] An absolute-$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ retention threshold (``$K_{\text{crit}}$'') ---
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}=19{,}666$ fails while $k_{\mathrm{eff}}=1{,}602$ holds; the boundary is
an $N\times$density interaction.
\item[R6] Retention failure mode as mis-selection --- inverted by the data; starvation
is the only currently significant mechanism (pending inject, \S\ref{sec:retention}).
\item[R7] Super-linear Python overhead as the memory-wall mechanism --- the mechanism is
allocator fragmentation (\S\ref{sec:systems}).
\item[R8] Pass-3 as the kernel bottleneck --- Pass-2 dominates ($74.4\%$ of M4,
Table~\ref{tab:perpass}).
\end{description}

\section{Conclusion and future work}
\label{sec:future}

We assembled, around the single object of softmax concentration, an exact ranking
identity (Theorem~1), a measured and descriptively-stated scaling law (Theorem~3) with
two independent confirmations, a pre-registered retention characterisation with a clean
leakage dissociation, a runtime-checkable per-query certificate, and a systems result in
which a fused kernel is a structural prerequisite. Every claim was held to a
matched-control discipline born from a falsified spectral hypothesis.

\paragraph{Pre-registered next steps (not yet claimed).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{Gap-1 --- causal upgrade of Theorem~3.} A matched-magnitude causal test of
the entropy-concentration premise; the protocol and both pre-registered outcomes are
specified below.
\item \textbf{Retention gates.} Multi-seed the $128\text{K}$ boundary; settle
starvation vs.\ mis-selection by oracle-inject; validate the needle-in-top-$k$ metric by
anti-inject; decompose latency into TTFT (dense prefill, not accelerated) and per-token
decode.
\item \textbf{Cross-model $\alpha$.} Measure $\alpha$ across softmax-scaling regimes
(vanilla+RoPE, SSMax, YaRN, $\alpha$-entmax) to convert ``model-specific'' from a caveat
into a law.
\item \textbf{Low-rank Q-axis (DCR-Loki).} Apply Theorem~1 in the top-$r$ PCA subspace
of $K$; reducing $d=128\to r=8$ targets a $\sim16\times$ data-movement reduction on top
of the $50\%$ compute saving, with a TV-distance error budget.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Registered protocol (Gap-1), recorded as of this release.} We register the
causal test of Theorem~\ref{thm:scaling} before running it, so that this release stakes
the test design. Object: the premise that entropy concentration is causally
load-bearing. For per-query logits $\boldsymbol{\ell}=QK^\top/\sqrt d$,
$p=\mathrm{softmax}(\boldsymbol{\ell})$, $H(p)=-\sum_i p_i\log p_i$, with entropy
gradient $g=\nabla_{\boldsymbol{\ell}}H=-p\odot(\log p+H)$:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{Treatment} $\delta_T$: a step along $+g/\|g\|$ sized so the realised
$\Delta H\approx\Delta_{\text{target}}$ nats (destroying concentration toward uniform).
\item \textbf{Matched control} $\delta_C$: a random direction projected onto
$\{v:\langle v,\hat g\rangle=0\}$ and scaled to $\|\delta_C\|=\|\delta_T\|$, so $H$ is
preserved to first order. The matched invariant is the \emph{perturbation norm}
$\|\delta\boldsymbol{\ell}\|$ --- not $\|\boldsymbol{\ell}\|$ --- which is precisely the
invariant the spectral-mechanism study of \S\ref{sec:method} got wrong before
correction.
\item \textbf{Contract gates} (else a cell is invalid): verified ratio
$\|\delta_T\|/\|\delta_C\|=1.000\pm10^{-3}$ and measured $|\Delta H_C|\le0.1\,
\Delta_{\text{target}}$.
\item \textbf{Random-$K$ baseline}: whether untrained $K$ yields the same $\alpha$,
contextualising algebraic vs.\ trained origin.
\item \textbf{Primary metric}: paired next-token NLL differential
$D_{\mathrm{NLL}}=\mathrm{NLL}_{\text{treat}}-\mathrm{NLL}_{\text{ctrl}}$; statistics by
$10$k-resample bootstrap $95\%$ CI and Wilcoxon signed-rank, mirroring
Table~\ref{tab:matched}; design $12$ layer--head pairs $\times\,25$ prompts, multi-seed
before any final claim.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Pre-registered outcomes (fixed now).} If the $95\%$ CI of $D_{\mathrm{NLL}}$
brackets zero \emph{and} Wilcoxon $p>0.05$: Theorem~\ref{thm:scaling} is finalised as
\emph{descriptive} under a matched-control null. If the CI is strictly positive
\emph{and} $p<0.05$: the concentration premise is \emph{causally grounded}. No
reinterpretation of a null as a weak positive is permitted; the decision is read off the
CI and Wilcoxon test. The result will be published as version~\texttt{v1.1} (versioned
DOI; concept DOI unchanged), by uncommenting the corresponding branch already prepared in
\S\ref{sec:theory}.

\section{Proof of Theorem~1}
\label{app:proof-thm1}
For any $Q\ne0$, $\langle Q,K\rangle=\|Q\|_2\,\langle \uQ,K\rangle$ with
$\uQ=Q/\|Q\|_2$ and $\|Q\|_2>0$. Hence for any $i,j$, $\langle Q,K_i\rangle-\langle
Q,K_j\rangle=\|Q\|_2(\langle\uQ,K_i\rangle-\langle\uQ,K_j\rangle)$, and the sign is
preserved because $\|Q\|_2>0$. The softmax is strictly monotone in its logits, so the
score ordering equals the projection ordering exactly. Ties of measure zero in exact
arithmetic appear at finite rate ($\sim12\%$ $z$-ties) under \texttt{bf16} and are
resolved by a stable tie-break (smaller index wins), which is the only source of the
$\sim10^{-3}$ residual in the $45/45$ brute-force check (\S\ref{sec:scaling}).

\section{Proof sketch of Theorem~3, and the kernel plan}
\label{app:proof-thm3}
\paragraph{Proof sketch.} The coverage floor admits
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}=\lfloor c_{\mathrm{floor}}N\rfloor$ keys. Under
Assumption~\ref{ass:entropy}, $\Keff(N)\sim N^{\alpha}$, so
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}/\Keff\sim N^{1-\alpha}\to\infty$. By Theorem~1 the selected window is
exactly the top-$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ subset by attention score, so the captured
probability mass $\to1$. By the head--tail decomposition,
$\|\Attn-\Attn_{k_{\mathrm{eff}}}\|_2=\tau_N\|\mu_{\text{tail}}-\mu_{\text{head}}\|_2$
with $\tau_N=\TV(P_N,\hat P_N)=O(N^{-(1-\alpha)})$; translating the output perturbation
to perplexity via the cross-entropy expansion preserves the rate. The worst-head
exponent $\alpha^*$ controls the rate; head-averaged $\alpha$ lower-bounds it.

\paragraph{Kernel engineering plan (M0--M7).} A FlashDecoding-style kernel: KV in HBM;
decode streams over KV in 64-key blocks, projecting onto $\uQ$ and maintaining a running
top-$k$ via warp-shuffle merge; selected $K_{\text{sel}},V_{\text{sel}}$ pass through a
fused online-softmax output kernel. The Pareto-optimal target tile regime is
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}\in[0.3N,0.5N]$ (\S\ref{sec:scaling}). The ABKV variant replaces
decode-time selection with a prefill prefix sort (\S\ref{sec:systems}); a Triton
bitonic top-$k$ (M6) is retained as a fallback if the prefix mechanism fails to transfer
from synthetic to real attention distributions.

\section{Production artifact: sort library and explainable dispatch}
\label{app:artifact}
The framework ships as a C++17/AVX-2 sort library (validated to $21.6\times$ over
\texttt{std::sort} on AVX-2, with bit-exact gradient verification
$\|\nabla\|_2=\sqrt{n}$ to machine precision and $36/36$ correctness targets) and an
AVX-512 build stress-tested at $\approx2{,}800$ correctness operations (one performance
gate failure, no correctness failures), a $528$-case adversarial sweep with zero
failures, byte-identical determinism over $50$ repeats, and linear scaling validated to
$64$M elements. The runtime exposes an \emph{explainable} dispatch surface: the strategy,
inversion density, NaN flag, and threads used per call are queryable, so adversarial
tests can assert \emph{which} path ran, not merely that the output was correct ---
``runtime decisions become falsifiable.'' This is the same observe-before-decide
discipline as \S\ref{sec:method}, realised in software.

\section{Matched-control raw and the Detector~v2 dispatcher}
\label{app:detector}
The matched-control run is the $300$-measurement design of \S\ref{sec:method}: twelve
layer--head pairs, $25$ prompts, context $256$, three forward passes per measurement
(unablated, dominant-mode treatment, matched bulk-mode control). Detector~v2 partitions
queries by a coherence score $s_{v2}$ and an axis confidence $c$ into Dense /

false negatives over the $45$ calibration cells of \S\ref{sec:certificate}. The
multi-axis extension predicts the axis count $r^\star=\lceil
1.142\,d_{\mathrm{eff}}(Q)-1.048\rceil$ (Pearson $0.9925$ over $40$ trials, $32$--$86\%$
relative error reduction over single-axis), promoting $d_{\mathrm{eff}}(Q)$ from a regime
selector to a quantitative axis-count predictor and matching the structural negative
result (Proposition~5.13: sparsity in the query spectrum does not imply rank-local
recoverability under single-axis PCA; realised as a controlled $-17$\,pt BERT-MNLI drop).
